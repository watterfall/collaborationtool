## Design Context

### Users

Research scientists, PhD students, postdocs, PIs, reviewers and interdisciplinary collaborators who move between rough ideas, prototypes, evidence, manuscripts, review and publication. They often work under fragile incentive systems: pressure to publish quickly, weak credit for early ideas or review labor, reproducibility overhead, privacy constraints, and cross-tool fragmentation.

The job to be done is not merely "write a paper". The product must help researchers lower the barrier to exploration, preserve half-formed scientific work, connect claims to evidence, collaborate with humans and AI, review with accountability, export standard scholarly formats, and keep control through local-first / self-hostable infrastructure.

### Brand Personality

Editorial, rigorous, local-first, bilingual, evidence-first. The interface should feel calm, scholarly and trustworthy, but still make room for scientific imagination: 3am ideas, contradictions, failed prototypes and lineage should feel like legitimate research artifacts.

### Aesthetic Direction

Warm paper background, serif-led editorial typography, thin rules, restrained ink colors, and no generic SaaS card grids. The site is light-first and manuscript-like, with Chinese and English treated as equal citizens. Provenance is part of the layout, not a popup. AI is a collaborator inside the work, not a chat sidebar.

### Design Principles

1. Local-first beats cloud-first for research trust.
2. Provenance, evidence and attribution must be visible product surfaces.
3. Ideas, prototypes and papers are separate but connected research spaces.
4. AI actions must be permissioned, auditable and human-approved.
5. Bilingual scientific work is first-class, not a translation afterthought.
6. Use editorial structure: hairlines, type hierarchy and rhythm instead of filled cards, shadows or saturated color.
